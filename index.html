About the artist

Sky Hayes is a glass artist based in Western Massachusetts whose work centers on the revival of pressed glass as a contemporary craft. With more than a decade of experience working in hot glass, Hayes has explored sculptural work, blown vessels, and traditional glass techniques. His current focus is creating pressed glass medallions that commemorate towns, historical events, and community traditions throughout New England.

Each medallion is made using a custom brass stamp and molten glass, pressed by hand in small batches. The process draws inspiration from historical pressed glass while adapting it into a modern format that allows towns and organizations to celebrate their identity in a tangible way.

Hayes developed this project out of a desire to connect glassmaking with local history and community life. By collaborating with town committees and local groups, he aims to create meaningful objects that honor regional heritage while helping keep the tradition of pressed glass alive.

Use cases for custom pressed glass medallions

Pressed glass medallions can be adapted for a wide range of personal, artistic, and commercial applications. Each piece is made to order and can be designed around your image, logo, symbol, or commemorative concept.

Awards and recognition

Ideal for art awards, community honors, staff recognition, or special presentations. A custom pressed medallion creates a more lasting and distinctive impression than a standard plaque or trophy.

Commemorative coins and medallions

Perfect for marking special events, anniversaries, memorials, festivals, historic landmarks, or limited edition releases. These can serve as collectible objects with strong visual and sentimental value.

Jewelry inserts and wearable components

Smaller medallions can be incorporated into jewelry, pendants, bolo ties, or mixed media adornment. They work especially well for artists, designers, and brands looking for a handcrafted centerpiece element.

Corporate gifts and branded objects

A custom glass medallion can showcase a business logo, institution seal, or symbolic design in a way that feels elevated, tactile, and memorable. Great for client gifts, milestone celebrations, or branded presentation pieces.

Artist editions and special releases

Designed for galleries, collectors, and limited runs, medallions can function as stand-alone artworks or as part of a broader series. This is a strong option for artists looking to create collectible objects with a custom identity.

Wedding and event keepsakes

Custom medallions can commemorate weddings, family events, retreats, or private gatherings. They offer a meaningful alternative to mass-produced favors and can be tailored to the tone of the occasion.

Frequently asked questions

What sizes are available?

Sizes can vary depending on the design and intended use. Small medallions may be appropriate for jewelry or inserts, while larger pieces work well for awards, commemorative objects, or display. If you have a target size in mind, include that in your inquiry.

What is the price range?

Pricing depends on size, complexity, quantity, and whether a custom stamp or mold is required. Small and simple pieces are generally more affordable, while larger or more detailed commissions require additional labor and setup. Reach out with your idea for an accurate quote.

What is the lead time?

Lead time depends on project scope and current studio schedule. Small runs may be completed relatively quickly, while larger custom orders or new stamp development may take longer. If you have a deadline, mention it when you reach out.

What image formats do you accept?

Clear black-and-white reference artwork works best for many designs. Vector files such as AI, EPS, or SVG are ideal when available, but high-resolution PNG, PDF, or JPEG files can also be useful depending on the project. If you are unsure whether your file will work, send it over and I can review it.

Can you work from a logo, drawing, or photograph?

Yes, depending on the image. Bold, high-contrast imagery usually translates best into pressed glass. Some artwork may need to be simplified or adjusted to work successfully in the final piece.

Do you ship?

Yes. Finished work can be carefully packed and shipped. Shipping cost depends on the size of the order, destination, and packaging requirements.

Is there a minimum order quantity?

Not always. Single custom pieces may be possible, and larger quantities are also available depending on the project.

Can you help refine my design?

Yes. If you have a concept but not a finalized file, I can often help determine what will translate best into glass.
